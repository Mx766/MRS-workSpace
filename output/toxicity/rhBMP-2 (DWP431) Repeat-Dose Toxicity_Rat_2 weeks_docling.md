Toxicokinetics of DWP431 in SD rats

## 1. SUMMARY

The objective of this study was conducted to assess the cumulative toxicity and safety of the test substance, DWP431, when intravenously injected once daily to six-week-old male and female Sprague-Dawley  (Crl:CD(SD)),  rats  for two weeks. Three test groups were designated as Groups 2, 3 and 4, at dose levels of 0.05, 0.18 and 0.5 mg/kg and one control group, Group 1 (DWP431 vehicle). Each group consisted of 10 males and 10 females. Three animals were added to each group for the toxicokinetic groups, Groups 5 and 8.

Parameters evaluated included; clinical signs, body weights, food consumption, ophthalmological examinations, urinalysis, hematology, blood chemistry, gross post mortem examinations, organ weights and histopathological evaluations of selected tissues. And the systemic exposure of the test substances was verified through the toxicokinetics.

All  animals  survived  the  duration  of  the  study.  During  the  dosing  period,  no  clinical abnormalities were observed in any animal of any dosing group.

No  test  substance-related  effects  on  body  weights,  food  consumption,  ophthalmological examinations, hematology, blood chemistry and gross necropsy were evident in any animal of any dosing group.

Absolute and relative ovary weights were decreased in females in the 0.18 and 0.5 mg/kg groups. The incidence of changes was small and a reduction of corpora lutea of the ovary was evident microscopically. However, they could not be determined as notable toxicological  findings  since  the  morphological  change  of  the  ovary  was  due  to  expected pharmacologic activity of the test substance.

Histopathologically,  a  cartilage  formation  and  increase  of  fibroblast  around  tail  vein  were noted  in  males  and  females  of  the  dosing  groups.  The  lesions  at  the  injection  site  were related  to  the  known  pharmacological  effect  of  the  test  substance.  Focal  appearance  of multinucleated cell of the lung parenchyma was noted in males in the 0.5 mg/kg group. It was  considered  as  natural  phagocytosis  by  ingestion  of  heterologous  protein  because  the main ingredient of the test substance is human genetic recombinant protein.

On the basis  of  toxicokinetic  results,  the  systemic  exposure  parameters  (AUC last ,  C max )  of DWP431 were tended to increase non-linearly with increasing doses in males and females in all  dosing  groups  and  did  not  reveal  any  difference  in  both  sexes.  The  systemic  exposure parameters  did  not  reveal  any  difference  in  repeated  dosing  and  the  T 1/2 values  were increased similarly as dosing level increased.

Based  on  these  result  of  the  2-week  toxicity  study  on  DWP431  following  intravenous injection  to  rats,  the  No  Observed  Adverse  Effect  Level  (NOAEL)  was  considered  to  be greater than 0.5 mg/kg in male and female rats under the conditions of this study.

## 2. OUTLINE OF STUDY

## 2.1 Purpose

The objective of this study was to assess the potential toxicity and safety of the test substance,  DWP431,  when  injected  intravenously  to  Sprague-Dawley  rats  once  daily for  two  weeks.  In  addition,  a  toxicokinetic  profiling  was  conducted  to  monitor  the systemic exposure of the test substance.
